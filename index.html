What is The Implant?

The implant is a small, flexible plastic rod about the size of a matchstick. It's placed under the skin of your upper arm by a healthcare provider.

Additional Information

The implant is made of a soft, flexible plastic called EVA (ethylene vinyl acetate). It contains a hormone called etonogestrel that is slowly released into your body over three years.

The insertion procedure takes only a few minutes and is done with local anesthesia. You can feel the implant under your skin, but it's not visible to others.
